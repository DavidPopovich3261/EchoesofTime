The Origins of Atlantis

Exploring the beginning of the greatest mystery in history.

Plato's Dialogues

The story of Atlantis was first told by the ancient Greek philosopher Plato around 360 B.C. He described it as a powerful and advanced kingdom that sank, in a single day and night of misfortune, into the ocean. According to the texts, Atlantis was larger than Libya and Asia combined.

"Now in this island of Atlantis there was a great and wonderful empire which had rule over the whole island and several others."

The Divine Connection

Legend says the island was created by Poseidon, the god of the sea. He carved the mountain where his love dwelt into a palace and enclosed it with three circular moats of increasing width, separated by rings of land. The Atlanteans built bridges northward from the mountain, making a route to the rest of the island.

Click to reveal: What language did they speak?

While we don't know for sure, some linguists theorize it might have been a precursor to Indo-European languages. However, in fiction, "Atlantean" is often depicted as a constructed language with unique symbols.

Possible Locations

Many believe Atlantis was located near the Pillars of Hercules (modern-day Gibraltar).

Key Terminology

Pillars of Hercules
The ancient name for the promontories that flank the entrance to the Strait of Gibraltar.
Orichalcum
A metal mentioned by Plato that was said to be mined in Atlantis, second only to gold in value.
